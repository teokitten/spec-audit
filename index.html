OpenAPI Spec Audit

Paste an OpenAPI 3.0/3.1 spec to get a scored report of its documentation gaps – missing descriptions, absent examples, undocumented error responses, loose type constraints, and semantic inconsistencies. Checks are configurable, reports are exportable, and you can compare runs to track whether a spec is improving or regressing.

This tool audits documentation quality, not schema correctness; it doesn't render your spec as reference docs.

This is a documentation-quality heuristic, not a spec validator – it checks whether a reader has what they need, not schema correctness. Checks turned off in "Configure checks" are excluded from scoring entirely, not just hidden from the report.

Known limitations
  • Short descriptions: brief but correct text (e.g. "Not Found") may still get flagged just for being short.
  • Field-name mentions: looks for field names in text – easily confused by lists of allowed values, so off by default.
  • Same word, different meaning: a term used inconsistently on purpose vs. by mistake isn't always distinguishable.
  • Bottom line: automated guesses, not certainties – use them to guide a review, not replace one.
